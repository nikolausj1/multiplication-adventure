Privacy Policy

Multiplication Adventure · Last updated July 7, 2026

Multiplication Adventure (“the app”) is a math game for children. We built it for our own kids, and we designed it to respect your family’s privacy completely. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with information. The short version: the app collects nothing and sends nothing anywhere.

Information we collect

None. The app does not collect, store off-device, transmit, or share any personal information about you or your child. There are no user accounts, no sign-in, and no way to enter an email address, phone number, or other contact detail.

Information stored on the device

The app saves your child’s progress — things like the player name or nickname you choose, chosen avatar, stars earned, and which facts have been practiced — only on the iPad itself. This information never leaves the device. It is not uploaded to us or to anyone else. Deleting the app removes it.

Internet and third parties

The app works entirely offline. It does not connect to the internet, does not contain advertising, and does not include any third-party analytics, tracking, or advertising software. We do not use cookies or any cross-app or cross-website tracking.

Children’s privacy

Because the app collects no data at all, it does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your country). It is designed to comply with the U.S. Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and Apple’s guidelines for apps directed at kids.

Purchases

The app contains no in-app purchases and no subscriptions.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new “last updated” date.
